-The United States is a federal republic made up of fifty states, a federal district, and several territories.
-The government is divided into three branches: the executive, the legislative, and the judicial.
-The Constitution is the supreme law of the land.
-The president is the head of state and the commander-in-chief of the armed forces.
-The legislature, made up of the Senate and the House of Representatives, makes laws.
-The judicial branch interprets the laws.
-The United States has a capitalist economy.
-Private individuals and businesses own property and produce goods and services.
-People earn money by working and selling goods and services.
-People can spend their money on what they want.
-The government regulates the economy.
-The United States is a melting pot.
-People from all over the world have come to the United States.
-They have brought their customs and traditions with them.
-The United States is made up of many different cultures.
-In 1859, British naturalist Charles Darwin published On the Origin of Species, which proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection.
-Darwin's theory was based on the observations that populations of organisms change over time, and that these changes are heritable.
-Natural selection is the process by which heritable traits that confer higher fitness are more likely to be passed on to future generations.
-Over time, this process can lead to the evolution of new species.
-Darwin's theory was initially met with skepticism, but it is now widely accepted by the scientific community.
