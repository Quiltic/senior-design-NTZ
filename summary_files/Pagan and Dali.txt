-In the early hours of December 7th, 1941, the Japanese navy launched a surprise attack on Pearl Harbor, Hawaii.
-The attack was a devastating blow to the US Pacific fleet, resulting in the sinking or damage of over 20 ships and the death of over 2,000 American servicemen and civilians.
-The US declared war on Japan the following day, and thus entered World War II.
-The Pearl Harbor attack would have far-reaching consequences for both the US and Japan, helping to shape the course of the war in the Pacific.
• The United States is a federal republic consisting of 50 states, a federal district (Washington, D.C.), five major territories (Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands, American Samoa, Guam, and the Northern Mariana Islands), and various minor islands. • The U.S. Constitution establishes a system of government with three separate branches: legislative, executive, and judicial. • The legislative branch (Congress) is responsible for making laws. The executive branch (the president and his administration) is responsible for carrying out the laws. The judicial branch (the courts) is responsible for interpreting the laws. • The president is the head of state and the head of the government. He is elected to a four-year term by the Electoral College. • The Congress is composed of the House of Representatives and the Senate. The House has 435 members, each representing a congressional district. The Senate has 100 members, two from each state. • The courts system consists of the Supreme Court (the highest court), the federal courts of appeals, the district courts (the lowest level of federal courts), and various state and local courts.
