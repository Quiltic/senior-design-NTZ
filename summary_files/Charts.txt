-In 1848, a group of German revolutionaries met in Frankfurt to draft a democratic constitution
-The Frankfurt Constitution was approved by the National Assembly in 1849, but was never put into effect
-The Frankfurt Constitution is significant because it was the first attempt to draft a democratic constitution for Germany
-The Constitution guaranteed basic rights and freedoms, such as freedom of speech and freedom of the press, and also established a parliamentary system of government
-The Frankfurt Constitution was ultimately unsuccessful because it was opposed by the German monarchs and was not ratified by the German states
