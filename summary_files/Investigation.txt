-The following is a list of topics that could potentially be covered in a high school history course on the Civil War era:
-The pre-Civil War era, including the events leading up to the war, such as the Missouri Compromise, the Nullification Crisis, and the Mexican-American War
-The Civil War itself, including the major battles, the Emancipation Proclamation, and the assassination of Abraham Lincoln
-The Reconstruction era, including the 13th, 14th, and 15th Amendments to the Constitution, and the rise of the Ku Klux Klan
-This is by no means an exhaustive list, but simply a starting point for further exploration.
-In order to make a perfect scrambled egg, start with a fresh egg.
-Crack the egg into a bowl and whisk it until the yolk and white are combined.
-Add a splash of milk and whisk again.
-Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat and melt a knob of butter.
-Pour in the egg mixture and, using a spatula, scramble until the eggs are cooked to your liking.
-Season with salt and pepper and serve.
