-The glass ceiling is a term that is used to describe the unseen, yet unbreakable barrier that keeps women from rising to the upper echelons of the corporate ladder.
-The term was first coined in the late 1960s by two female journalists working for The New York Times, and has since been widely used to describe the barriers faced by women in the workplace.
-There is no one single reason why the glass ceiling exists, but some of the contributing factors include gender-based discrimination, sexual harassment, and the lack of flexible work arrangements that allow women to juggle work and family responsibilities.
-Despite the challenges, there are a number of successful women who have broken through the glass ceiling and achieved top leadership positions in their fields.
