-In the late 1800s, a group of wealthy industrialists in the United States began to worry that the country was becoming too democratic
-They believed that the “common man” was not qualified to make decisions about complex issues, and that the government should be run by a small group of wealthy, intelligent men
-They started a movement called “Progressivism” to try to bring about change
-Progressives believed in using the government to improve society, and they were successful in passing a number of reforms, including laws regulating child labor, food and drugs, and working conditions
-The Progressive movement lost momentum in the 1920s, but its ideas are still influential today]
- The American Revolution was a time of great upheaval and change in the thirteen American colonies. - Colonists sought to break away from British rule for a variety of reasons, including high taxes, lack of representation in Parliament, and restrictions on trade. - The Revolution began with the Boston Tea Party in 1773, and escalated into the Declaration of Independence in 1776. - The Revolutionary War was fought between the American colonies and the British army, culminating in the surrender of the British at Yorktown in 1781. - The American Revolution resulted in the formation of the United States of America, a new nation founded on the principles of liberty and justice.
