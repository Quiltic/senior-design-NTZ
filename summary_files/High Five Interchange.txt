-In the early morning hours of April 15, 1912, the Titanic, one of the largest and most luxurious ocean liners ever built, struck an iceberg and began to sink
-The Titanic was carrying more than 2,200 passengers and crew, and over 1,500 people perished in the disaster
-The sinking of the Titanic was one of the deadliest maritime disasters in history and remains one of the most infamous
-The Titanic has been the subject of numerous books, films, and other works of popular culture
-In the early 1800s, America was mostly rural and agricultural, with a few small towns and cities.
-Most people lived on farms and in small villages.
-There was little industry, and what there was, was mostly in the Northeast.
-Transportation was slow and difficult, so people tended to stay in one place.
-The population was largely English-speaking, but there were also German, Irish, and Dutch immigrants.
-The American frontier was the westward expansion of the United States into the unclaimed lands west of the Appalachian Mountains.
-The frontier was a symbol of opportunity and hope for many Americans.
-The frontier was also a dangerous place, where people had to struggle against the elements, Native Americans, and outlaws.
