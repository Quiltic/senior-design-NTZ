-In 1848, a group of German socialists met in London to discuss the Communist Manifesto
-In 1864, another group of German socialists met in London and founded the International Workingmen's Association, also known as the First International
-The First International was a federation of trade unions and socialist parties
-In 1871, the Paris Commune was established
-The Paris Commune was a municipal government that lasted for two months before being suppressed by the French army
-In 1876, the First International was dissolved
-In 1889, the Second International was founded
-The Second International was a federation of socialist parties
-In 1914, the First World War broke out
-In 1917, the Russian Revolution occurred
-In 1918, the German Revolution occurred
-In 1919, the Third International was founded
-The Third International was a communist organization
-In 1922, the Soviet Union was established
-In 1933, the Nazi Party came to power in Germany
-In 1939, the Second World War broke out
-In 1945, the Third International was dissolved
