-The glass ceiling is a term that is used to describe the unseen, yet unbreakable barrier that keeps women from rising to the upper echelons of the corporate ladder.
-The term was first coined in the late 1970s and early 1980s, and has been used to describe the barriers that women face in the workforce ever since.
-The glass ceiling is often seen as a symptom of the larger problem of gender inequality in the workplace.
-There are a number of factors that contribute to the existence of the glass ceiling, including discrimination, unconscious bias, and the lack of women in leadership positions.
-Despite the challenges, there are a number of women who have been able to break through the glass ceiling and reach the top of their respective fields.
